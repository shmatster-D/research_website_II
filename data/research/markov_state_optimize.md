Deterministic trajectories can be traced through a computationally discretized state space to yield discrete approximations to the evolution of the system's Fokker-Planck equation through a two-dimensional operator. However, most implementations of this technique require a large amount of saved data. 

We are developing a method for reducing the amount of data required to compute an operator which effectively captures the steady-state statistics of the Lorenz attractor, building on the modified Leicht-Newman algorithm to identify the most salient regions in state space which best represent the steady-state statistics of the system, recovering these as the only saved states. We are also evaluating this technique against operators constructed using other methods of partitioning state space.
