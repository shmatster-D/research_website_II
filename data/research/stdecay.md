Noninteracting point particles in homogeneous isotropic turbulence (HIT) cluster in unique patterns based on their dimensionless mass ratio and Stokes numbers. How these patterns change as the turbulence undergoes self-decay is not well understood. In this project, we looked at high fidelity, one-way coupled simulations of point particles in HIT, and quantified their clustering, developing a length-scale-aware metric and scaling laws to account for the growing length scale of the decaying turbulence and its effect on the particle clustering.
